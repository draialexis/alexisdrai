I'm quitting smoking : my journal

  • Day 0 - nov. 1, 2020

    02:00 AM (so, 02/11/2020 technically) -- I smoked my last cigarette less than an hour ago. I threw the unfinished pack in the trash and started setting up this log. If you're reading this, I'm probably fine, and I'm thankful that you're taking the time. My approach was to consult a general practitioner after doing some research on effective cessation methods. I was recommended the usual nicotine substitute method, and therefore I'll be using patches all November. If the going gets tough, I also have special lozenges that are supposed to help. I'm not planning on using those -- place your bets! In the first few months, you can expect short daily updates here, linked and announced on Twitter. Wish me luck!

  • Day 1 - nov. 2, 2020

    00:30 AM -- Well, fuck me. I'm using a patch so it could probably have been worse, but this is harder than I thought. I'm restless, time seems to pass slowly, and I'm warm and sweaty and distracted. But I made it so far. Now let's see if I can sleep.

    In retrospect, and as I mentioned above, it's also easier than it could have been in some ways. One other reason is the support I'm getting from knowing that some people care and are paying attention to my resolution and my progress: some family members, some perfect strangers, some friends, and even the woman I have a crush on. They know, I know that they know -- there's no going back without disappointing basically everybody. And that's all for the better. Also, I totally used my first nicotine lozenge on night #1 like some sort of chump.

    10:30 PM -- I started today with the satisfaction of knowing I can get through a day and night. Still restless, but pretty effective at brute forcing through stuff. I cleaned my whole place up and rearranged it. May well wash the walls tomorrow.

    At this point, I don't think I'll smoke again, but I do think quitting could burn me out or bring me down if I don't manage the process intelligently.
