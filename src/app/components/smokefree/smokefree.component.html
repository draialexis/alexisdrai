I'm quitting smoking : my journal

  • Day 0 - nov. 1, 2020

    02:00 AM (so, 02/11/2020 technically) -- I smoked my last cigarette less than an hour ago. I threw the unfinished pack in the trash and started setting up this log. If you're reading this, I'm probably fine, and I'm thankful that you're taking the time. My approach was to consult a general practitioner after doing some research on effective cessation methods. I was recommended the usual nicotine substitute method, and therefore I'll be using patches all November. If the going gets tough, I also have special lozenges that are supposed to help. I'm not planning on using those -- place your bets! In the first few months, you can expect short daily updates here, linked and announced on Twitter. Wish me luck!

    00:30 AM (so, 03/11/2020 technically) -- Well, fcuk me. I'm using a patch so it could probably have been worse, but this is harder than I thought. I'm restless, time seems to pass slowly, and I'm warm and sweaty and distracted. But I made it so far. Now let's see if I can sleep.

    In retrospect, and as I mentioned above, it's also easier than it could have been in some ways. One other reason is the support I'm getting from knowing that some people care and are paying attention to my resolution and my progress: some family members, some perfect strangers, some friends, and even the woman I have a crush on. They know, I know that they know -- there's no going back without disappointing basically everybody. And that's all for the better. Also, I totally used my first nicotine lozenge on night #1 like some sort of chump.

  • Day 6 - nov. 7, 2020

    04:30 PM -- So, today was pretty fcuking weird. To be fair, this whole year's been unpredictable and disorienting for most humans, this autumn especially.

    I spent a few hours crying in my bed today. I've also thought about why that happened. And I mean, sure, many citizens have been showing complacency when faced with fascism and authoritarianism at home and abroad. Governments have been displaying too little willingness to preserve an inhabitable world for our species. Old, bad, dead ideas, like market protectionism and eugenics, have been resurfacing.

    And maybe it looks like I'm straying out of scope for this smoking cessation log, but I don't think so. The thing is, I used to have a number of outlets for this 21st century funk many of us are feeling right now. Some of those outlets are gone: I'm away from those I care about, I'm stuck inside, and I won't smoke anymore. I hadn't cried in a very long time, and right now I feel that it might be because I used to have those other outlets. My will has been wavering, and yeah, I've thought about how I could maybe forget about quitting for now, and even hide or lie to y'all about it (!), but I'm still in the game and I'm happy to report: it'll take a bit more that the above list of grievances to make me take that fcuking step back.

  • Day 8 - nov. 9, 2020

    6:30 PM -- It's been hard to focus for about eight days (weird, right?). And it was more important today. I've had a hard time getting to study, or sticking to it more than a few minutes, and it showed during my exam performance today.

    Now, I don't want to dramatise. I didn't do that bad, I don't think. Also, I will hopefully be in better shape by the time the finals roll up, in January, which should help my average grades.

    Addiction-wise, I've been thinking about smoking -- as in, the physical act. Picturing it, remembering the sensations. I don't want it. There's a lot I really don't miss about it. In a way, it's something that became a habit and a need for me after I got used to the smell, to the physical reactions, and all that. There was a barrier to entry, in that way, and that barrier has come back at some point this month. All that being said, I don't miss smoking per se, but I do miss something as a result of quitting. Part of the challenge is going to be to form new habits that are better, or neutral. Food for my addled thoughts...
