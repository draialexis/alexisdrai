    02:00 AM (so, 02/11/2020 technically) -- I smoked my last cigarette less than an hour ago. I threw the unfinished pack in the trash and started setting up this log. If you're reading this, I'm probably fine, and I'm thankful that you're taking the time. My approach was to consult a general practitioner after doing some research on effective cessation methods. I was recommended the usual nicotine substitute method, and therefore I'll be using patches all November. If the going gets tough, I also have special lozenges that are supposed to help.

    06:30 PM -- It's been hard to focus for about eight days (weird, right?). And it was more important today. I've had a hard time getting to study, or sticking to it more than a few minutes, and it showed during my exam performance today.

    Now, I don't want to dramatise. I didn't do that bad, I don't think. Also, I will hopefully be in better shape by the time the finals roll up, in January, which should help my average grades.

    Addiction-wise, I've been thinking about smoking -- as in, the physical act. Picturing it, remembering the sensations. I don't want it. There's a lot I really don't miss about it. In a way, it's something that became a habit and a need for me after I got used to the smell, to the physical reactions, and all that. There was a barrier to entry, in that way, and that barrier has come back at some point this month. All that being said, I don't miss smoking per se, but I do miss something as a result of quitting. Part of the challenge is going to be to form new habits that are better, or neutral. Food for my addled thoughts...
